A great book for babies/toddlers is one that is fun to read again and again. Here’s a list of the ones our 15 month old seems to enjoy the most - often with smiles of recognition on certain pages, or pointing to favourite images. They’re all board books to handle the chewing era. In no particular order:

## Horton hears a Hoo

I love the rhythm of the words in this. It bounces along quite nicely and is completely hatstand in conceit. His use of the word ‘lad’ makes me feel qiote sure Seuss has (or should have) northern roots. [![small-2-of-3.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-2-of-3-5-768x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-2-of-3-jpg-6/)

## A Squash and a squeeze

A fun tale that feels like it ought to have been around for centuries. There is an underlying message about not being greedy, but it’s very subtle and not at all hectoring. [![small-9-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-9-of-16-2.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-9-of-16-jpg-3/)[![for-emailing-1](/assets/images/2016/09/for-emailing-1-855x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/for-emailing-1/)

## Goodnight Moon

A book for bedtime, this is about 60 years old and - I think - better known in North America. The pace always mesmerises our little one and she invariably sits through it without getting fidgety. [![small-3-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-3-of-16-3-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-3-of-16-jpg-4/)

## Mog the forgetful cat

Again, this one is usually a complete read-through, which is amazing given the images are pretty plain and the text is plentiful. But the story is lovely and there is the always enjoyable appearance of a big, furry, striped tiger in the middle [![small-2-of-8](/assets/images/2016/09/small-2-of-8-768x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/small-2-of-8/)

## Brown Bear, brown bear, what do you see

Simple, hypnotic, colourful and enjoyable to chant. [![small-3-of-8](/assets/images/2016/09/small-3-of-8-768x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/small-3-of-8/)

## Hairy Maclary from Donaldson’s Dairy

It won’t be long before you know the names and order of the doggies who go for an adventure. A stone cold classic - and our first exposure to the toughest tom in town. [![small-4-of-8](/assets/images/2016/09/small-4-of-8-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/small-4-of-8/)

## Tiddler

The #1 best kids book we’ve found so far. It’s read every night without fail. A brilliant story, fun to read and full of imagery and wit. [![small-6-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-6-of-16-1-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-6-of-16-jpg-2/)

## The very hungry caterpillar

A beautiful tale. It was only on the third reading that I realised I’d had this read to me as a kid. ![](/assets/images/2016/09/small-7-of-16-3.jpg)

## Hairy Maclary and Zachary Quack

More Hairy adventures. The wonderfully cyclical tale of a sensitive dog and a keen, but helpful duck. Some amazing writing too - the pace of the running is brilliantly told. ![](/assets/images/2016/09/small-8-of-16-3.jpg)

## Slinky Malinki Early Bird

More from the Maclary stable. This time the cameo cat gets a spinoff. A funny early morning tale that all cat owners will identify with [![for-emailing-3](/assets/images/2016/09/for-emailing-3-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/for-emailing-3/)

## The Paper Dolls

Makes me cry every time. The bittersweet tale of loss and nostalgia. Just amazing. [![small-5-of-8](/assets/images/2016/09/small-5-of-8-768x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/small-5-of-8/)

## Spot Loves his dad

Simple and lovely [![for-emailing-2](/assets/images/2016/09/for-emailing-2-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/for-emailing-2/)

## The tiger who came to tea

A total classic which, I believe anyway, is actually the recounting of Sophie’s dream about a tiger that she’s prompted to tell when she sees a stripey cat in the street. [![small-10-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-10-of-16-4-768x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-10-of-16-jpg-5/)

## The snowy day

Didn’t like this at first, but I’ve come to love the flow and wonder of this American tale of a magical snowy day. [![small-6-of-8](/assets/images/2016/09/small-6-of-8-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/junos-favourite-books/small-6-of-8/)

## Pantone colour books

These are six super simple, short books about colour. Each page has an item in that colour and our one year old loves them every time. [![small-13-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-13-of-16-3-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-13-of-16-jpg-4/)

## Stick man

More classic Donaldson - with a contrived but clever twist [![small-14-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-14-of-16-4-768x1024.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-14-of-16-jpg-5/)

## Room on the broom

I just love doing the witch voice (and not just when reading this book) [![small-15-of-16.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-15-of-16-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-15-of-16-jpg/)

## Zog

Girl power [![small-1-of-3.jpg](/assets/images/2016/09/small-1-of-3-2-1024x768.jpg)](http://www.carlosfandango.net/small-1-of-3-jpg-3/)
